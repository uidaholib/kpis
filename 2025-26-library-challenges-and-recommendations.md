# University of Idaho Library: Operational Challenges and Strategic Analysis 2025-26

## Executive Summary

This report presents an analysis of operational challenges facing the University of Idaho Library based on comprehensive review of 2025-26 team and unit final reports. While the library demonstrated strong performance across many areas — particularly in accessibility compliance, digital scholarship, first-year instruction, and collections assessment — several systemic issues, resource constraints, and organizational questions require strategic attention.

*Data sources: [teams-units-25-26.csv](_data/teams-units-25-26.csv), [final team reports](Team%20Reports%20Spring%202026/), and [unit reports](Unit%20Reports%20Spring%202026/)*

*Note: This draft is pending the SPEC unit final report. It will be updated when that report is received.*

## Key Achievements

### Accessibility as a Library-Wide Priority
Multiple teams and units converged on accessibility work this year, creating a cross-cutting accomplishment. The Digital Collections Team reprocessed over 3,800 PDFs for OCR and developed 300+ audio transcripts. The Website Team reviewed 100% of pages in navigation menus and developed PDF remediation approaches shared across campus. The Instruction Team reviewed all public LibGuides for WCAG 2.1 compliance. DSOS developed tools, workflows, and documentation for accessibility remediation. This coordinated effort positions the library well ahead of ADA requirements, even after the federal deadline extension.

### Collections Feedback and Data-Driven Decision Making
The Liaisons Team's collections feedback survey — 92 responses from faculty across all colleges — was a breakthrough in gathering qualitative data about how faculty use library resources. The resulting report, shared with the Collections Team, is directly informing collection development decisions, fund allocations, and Read & Publish agreement evaluations. This represents the most substantive liaison-collections collaboration in recent years.

### Digital Scholarship and Open Education
DSOS published 12+ digital scholarship projects, spent the full $25,000 OAPF allocation, launched the Open Invitation podcast, and joined the Library Publishing Coalition as part of University of Idaho Press development. The Open Engagement Team built a working K-12 education portal (thecdil.github.io/oep/) with inquiry kits tied to Idaho digital collections — a new form of community engagement.

### First-Year Experience Sustainability Improvements
The FYE Team shifted grading from librarians to instructors, incorporated AI literacy into instruction, and maintained high participation rates (80%+ submission, 83-99% full credit). These structural changes address the sustainability concerns raised in the 2024-25 report.

### Student Employee Development
The SED Team secured leadership approval for a revised pay tier structure and continued normalizing developmental supervision practices. The emphasis on relational supervision, check-ins, and student wellbeing represents a mature approach to student employment.

## Systemic Operational Challenges

### 1. Staffing Transitions and Key Person Dependencies

**Multiple critical departures:**
- Maryelizabeth Koepele (Digital Projects Manager) leaving July 2026 — impacts Website Team, Digital Collections Team, and Open Engagement Team
- Zoe Baum (Archives Coordinator) left January 2026, replaced by Elaina Pierson
- Curriculum Center Supervisor retiring — hours reduced from 6am-2am to 7am-midnight

**Sabbatical impacts:**
- Hanwen Dong on sabbatical fall 2026 — impacts Discovery Team (Pam stepping in as lead), Instruction Team (tutorial workflow paused), Website Team, and FYE tutorial software exploration

The concentration of departures in digital/technical roles creates risk for web maintenance, digital collections work, and accessibility remediation continuity.

### 2. Budget and Collections Pressures

The Collections Team and Discovery & Acquisitions Unit identified $228,217 in cancellations for the year, reducing expected inflation impact. However:
- Cancellation decisions are becoming more difficult as low-hanging fruit has been cut
- The print serials withdrawal project is massive: 7,605 items marked/withdrawn from 371 titles, with disposal ongoing through summer 2026
- "Letters from the Library" newsletter identified as low-impact for driving content engagement
- Resource sharing fill rates require investigation after Rapido implementation (Summit borrowing fill rate dropped from 95% to 83%)

### 3. Team Structure Questions

Several teams' final reports raise questions about organizational fit:

**Research Impact Team — Lead recommends sunsetting.** The initial work of developing VERSO policies and procedures is complete. Remaining work is increasingly technical (automated CV parsing, metadata scraping) rather than requiring cross-library consultation. UniVERSO is progressing without dedicated team oversight.

**MarCom Team — No progress on process refinement.** Fall and spring updates both note "no progress" on Objective #3 (refine processes). Newsletter identified as low-impact. The team maintained existing channels but did not advance strategically.

**Liaisons Team — Feedback mechanism objective didn't function as intended.** The team recommends dropping or restructuring this objective. The informal feedback that happens through dual-team membership (Jeremy on Collections, Jylisa on Instruction) is more effective than formalized cross-team meeting attendance.

**Discovery Team — Facing major transition.** The NDE migration (delayed to summer 2027) will fundamentally change the team's technical landscape, shifting from JavaScript to Angular/JSON modules with new analytics platforms. This is a significant technical challenge.

### 4. Physical Infrastructure

The PSPP Team's end-of-term walkthroughs documented:
- Outdated floor map signs on floors 1 and 2
- Minor wall damage in study room 322
- Paint touch-ups needed on 4th floor and several specific locations
- Water-damaged ceiling tiles across floors 1-4 (restrooms, Map Room, tutoring area, RS164 shelves, 4th floor elevator)
- "Marian Storm Walk-throughs" for post-rainstorm checks remain a challenge to implement

### 5. Assessment and Documentation Gaps

- SED Team: standardized reflection tools and documentation of student learning outcomes did not progress as hoped
- MarCom Team: social media engagement and newsletter statistics still not systematically tracked
- Several teams lack clear metrics for impact measurement
- Instruction Team spring data table was empty (spring 2026 metrics not reported)

## Strategic Recommendations

### Team Restructuring Considerations

**Teams to evaluate for sunset or rechartering:**
- **Research Impact Team**: Lead explicitly recommends sunsetting. VERSO management can continue through normal unit work.
- **MarCom Team**: Needs rechartering with clearer goals or absorption into a broader communications strategy.
- **Liaisons Team Objective 3**: Drop the formal feedback mechanism objective; the organic feedback through shared membership is sufficient.

**Teams with strong momentum to maintain:**
- **Open Engagement Team**: K-12 portal project has clear trajectory; needs to manage Maryelizabeth's departure
- **Mentorship/Scholarship Team**: Writing circle launch was a success; 3rd year review support is a clear next-year priority
- **Digital Collections Team**: Archive drive organization and continued accessibility remediation are multi-year commitments

### Staffing Priorities

1. **Digital Projects Manager replacement** (Maryelizabeth's role) — critical for website maintenance, digital collections, and accessibility work
2. **Cross-training for Hanwen's sabbatical coverage** — particularly for tutorial workflow and Discovery Team lead responsibilities
3. **ILL staffing** — Dakota Woodward's departure during a period of Rapido implementation and changing fill rates

### Technology and Infrastructure

- **NDE preparation**: Discovery Team's delayed adoption to summer 2027 is prudent; comparative analysis testing should continue
- **Website redesign**: Continue parallel work on new theme and content reorganization; the university's "marketing focused" theme is not appropriate for library information architecture
- **Pressbooks/Canvas integration**: FYE team's need to migrate ILWs from Articulate is urgent for accessibility compliance
- **AI services planning**: REL Unit commenced planning in May 2026; opportunity to coordinate with AI Team objectives

## Summary Assessment

The University of Idaho Library shows **strong coordinated performance** on accessibility, digital scholarship, and instruction while facing **specific structural challenges** around staffing transitions, team lifecycle management, and physical infrastructure maintenance.

The most significant strategic questions for 2026-27 are:
1. Which teams have completed their mission cycle and should be sunset or rechartered?
2. How will digital/technical staffing gaps be managed during a year of multiple departures and a sabbatical?
3. Can the collections feedback model be expanded into a sustainable cycle of faculty engagement?
4. How should the library prepare for the NDE transition while maintaining current discovery services?

The library's **core services remain strong and growing**. Challenges are primarily **organizational and transitional** rather than performance-based, suggesting targeted restructuring and strategic hiring will address the most pressing concerns.
